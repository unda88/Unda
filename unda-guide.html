How to use the suite
Complete trading workflow
⚖️ Risk Calc → 📐 Kelly → Trade → 📒 Log it → 🎲 Simulate → Optimise
⚖️
Risk Calculator
Use before every single trade
1
Portfolio size — Enter the total value of your trading account in USD.
2
Margin size — How much of your portfolio you allocate as margin. Start with 10-20% if you are new to leverage.
3
Risk % — The maximum % of your margin you are willing to lose on this trade. Most professional traders use 1-2%.
4
Stop loss % — Measure this with TradingView's measurement tool. Click on your entry candle and drag to your stop loss level.
5
Check the warning — Always verify on your exchange that your stop loss % is above the liquidation distance. The calculator warns you if leverage is too high.
Tip: The leverage number tells you exactly what to set on Bybit, Binance or OKX. The contracts number tells you the position size in USD.
📐
Kelly Criterion
Use weekly to optimise your risk per trade
1
Win probability — Use your real win rate from the Trade Logbook. Never estimate — only use real tracked data.
2
R:R ratio — Use your average R:R from the logbook statistics.
3
Always use Half-Kelly — The full Kelly is mathematically optimal but too aggressive for real trading. Divide by 2 for a more conservative and sustainable approach.
4
Negative Kelly — If Kelly returns a negative number, you have no mathematical edge with your current stats. Work on improving your win rate or R:R first.
📒
Trade Logbook
Log every trade immediately after closing
1
Log every trade — Win, loss or break even. Consistency is everything. One missing trade makes your statistics unreliable.
2
Profit factor — A profit factor above 1.5 is good. Above 2.0 is excellent. Below 1.0 means you are losing money overall.
3
Win rate vs R:R — A lower win rate is fine if your R:R is high. With a 1:2 R:R you only need to win 33% of trades to break even.
4
Use your real stats — After 30+ trades, use your real win rate and avg R:R as input for the Kelly Criterion and Trade Simulator.
🎲
Trade Simulator
Use to test scenarios before risking real money
1
Use real data — Input your real win rate and R:R from the logbook for realistic results.
2
Run multiple simulations — Set 10-20 simulations to see the range of possible outcomes. The spread between best and worst shows your variance.
3
Test your risk % — Try different Bet % values. Higher risk = higher potential gains but also higher ruin risk. The Kelly Criterion tells you the optimal value.
4
Ruin risk — If any simulation line hits $0, your bet size is too high for your win rate. Reduce your risk per trade.
Tip: Run a 1000 trade simulation with your real stats. If the worst case scenario still shows profit, your system is robust.
